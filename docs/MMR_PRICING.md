# Dota 2 MMR pricing model

## What the storefront now prices

- Exact public medal divisions: **Herald I–V** through **Divine I–V**, plus **Immortal**.
- MMR Boost pricing takes the larger scope of the selected MMR amount or the exact number of medal steps. A route such as Legend III → Ancient I is therefore not treated as the same request as Legend I → Ancient V.
- Solo Assist and Duo Queue are customer-operated. The customer keeps control of their own Steam account; credentials and remote access are never requested.

## Public-market check (reviewed July 2026)

Public competitor listings vary widely because delivery models differ. Neznakov advertises Dota MMR Boost from $1.25 and calibration from $13, while Skycoach lists Duo MMR Boost from $15. EliteBoost and BoostingMarket expose the familiar I–V divisions in their Dota selectors. Those are market floor signals, not like-for-like promises or a guarantee of delivery.

The storefront uses a conservative, lower CAD schedule for customer-operated delivery:

| Service | Starting scope | Storefront rate (CAD, before tax) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| MMR Boost | 100 MMR / one medal step | $8.25 per 100 MMR or $9.50 per medal step, whichever is greater |
| Duo Queue | MMR/Calibration | +20% |
| Calibration | each match | $10.00, with 10% off a 10-match block |
| Behavior Score | +500 score scope | $4.50 |
| Win Boost | each Duo Queue win | $7.50 |
| Coaching | private session | $39.00 |

Rank factor, package savings, selected tier, and delivery priority are shown in the live server quote. This keeps the site’s published price materially lower than the earlier configuration while preserving a transparent premium for high-medal queues.

The checkout enforces a $25.00 minimum order total before tax.

## Important rank note

Medal thresholds are estimates, not a promise of a fixed MMR boundary. Dota’s displayed rank/MMR relationship can change with updates and seasonal calibration, so the checkout captures both medal route and requested MMR scope.
